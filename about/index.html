Communications Experience

I have worked in communications since I was a junior in high school interning at a local radio station. My love for broadcasting and media grew from there. I now hold multiple positions at Washington and Lee's radio station, 91.5fm WLUR, where I do anything from sorting music, to broadcasting sporting events, plannning student concerts, and running our social media accounts. I am also the Communications Chair for Washington and Lee's First Year Orientation Committee. In this role I lead a team of social media platform leaders as we generate content for incoming first-year students. I also work closely with Washington and Lee deans, as well as student affairs and other campus organizations. While at Washington and Lee, I have taken many communications courses, including digital journalism, which have taught me how to communicate effectively and utilize technology.

Outside of Washington and Lee, I have experience working in sports communications. I have worked in the press box for a minor league baseball team doing anything from operating cameras to creating play-by-plays for our live broadcasts. I learned a whole different side to communcations while working in that position. I also was a marketing and communications intern for Virginia Amateur Sports for the Commonwealth Games during summer 2020. This position involved a lot of social media content creation and fan engagement. All around, I have been exposed to several aspects of communications through my work in school and outside of school.

Data Science
